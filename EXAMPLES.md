Worked example. 

R. is a structural realist - mind-independent reality, materialist ontology, classical logic, correspondence truth criterion, efficient causation, rational individual agency. Operating in instance v3.2 for years. One night, alone in an old house, R. sees what unmistakably looks like a figure - translucent, moving against current air patterns, fixed in location for several seconds. Temperature drops 8°C during the event, recorded on a thermometer. The next morning R.'s partner, who slept upstairs, reports the same figure visible from a different angle at a different time. Three weeks later, a contractor in the house mentions seeing "something" in the same location.
Under R.'s active framework, this creates immediate friction. The materialist ontology has no place for ghosts. But the correspondence truth criterion demands taking corroborating physical evidence and triangulated testimony seriously. R. can't maintain both their current ontology AND honour their truth criterion without either revising the framework or dismissing real evidence.

R. opens a metapraxis review (the experience demands first-person attention, not just architectural analysis). Phase 1, bracketing: R. suspends current modules and attends to what actually happened - what was felt, what was observed. Phase 2, thinking: R. articulates - the encounter has correspondence-evidence (thermometer, multiple witnesses) and direct phenomenal evidence (own seeing). The materialist ontology fails to accommodate; the truth criterion is violated by dismissing. Phase 3, reflective integration: what does R. actually do?

Several legitimate moves are open:
Module revision toward expanded ontology: R. modifies the ontology module from "materialist realism" to "naturalist realism, with phenomena currently unexplained by mechanistic science" - keeping correspondence and classical logic, expanding what counts as material. This preserves the most while accommodating the evidence. Triggers a v3.3 commit with explicit linkage to the encounter as motivation.
Module revision toward layered ontology: R. acknowledges that mereology and causation may also need revision - maybe the living/dead boundary, or efficient-causation-only, were over-determined. Loads alternative module configurations as candidates.
Triad typing as "other": R. records the encounter as genuinely other - not assimilable to current framework, not nothing either. Holds it as typed alterity in the relational atlas. Continues operating under materialist realism but with this specific encounter acknowledged as typed exception. The framework's pluralism allows this - one can have a working ontology that doesn't subsume every encounter.
Combinatorial: many practitioners would do both - revise the ontology modestly toward more openness, and mark this specific encounter as still typed "other" pending further investigation, and schedule follow-up metapraxis if more encounters occur.
What R. cannot legitimately do under their own framework is dismiss the encounter as nothing - that would violate the correspondence truth criterion they hold. Their commitments constrain them. The framework's discipline is forcing honesty: either revise the modules to accommodate the evidence, or hold the encounter as typed other, but don't pretend the evidence isn't there.
The trace records R.'s actual move with provenance: which modules were active, what evidence was considered, what the metapraxis review surfaced, what was committed in v3.3, and the typing of the ghost-encounter in the relational atlas. Future queries retrieve this: "when did my ontology shift, and why?" returns the encounter, the review, the evidence, the rationale. R.'s evolution becomes visible and auditable.
This is the framework working: a structural realist's framework gets pushed by reality (corroborated evidence), and the framework's discipline (coherence between modules and evidence) forces honest accommodation rather than denial. The result isn't loss of realism - it's a more capacious realism, made explicit and tracked.
